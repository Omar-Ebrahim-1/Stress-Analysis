\section{Introduction}
The report outlines the process the team followed to design and construct
the pressure vessel.\\[10pt]
The problem statement is to maximize the pressure that the vessel can withstand.
To achieve this goal, the team employed a structured approach in the design and
construction of the pressure vessel, relying on a combination of essential
tools and methodologies, including:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Sketches for initial conceptualization
    \item Detailed CAD Drawings for precise technical specifications
    \item Analytical Calculations to predict the performance of the design
    \item Rigorous testing procedures to refine and validate the design
\end{itemize}
These combined efforts were instrumental in ensuring the ultimate design's
robustness, reliability, and efficiency.

\section{Calculations}
For cylindrical thin walled pressure vessels, the hoop stress is given by:
\begin{equation}
    \sigma_1 = \frac{pr}{t}
    \label{eq:hoop}
\end{equation}
and the longitudinal stress is given by:
\begin{equation}
    \sigma_2 = \frac{pr}{2t}
    \label{eq:long}
\end{equation}
where $p$ is the internal pressure, $r$ is the radius of the vessel, and $t$ is
the wall thickness of the vessel.\\[10pt]
The pressure vessel is thin walled if the following condition is satisfied:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{r}{t} > 10
    \label{eq:thin}
\end{equation}
Since the pressure vessel's wall thickness must not exceed 4 mm, and the 
outside diameter of the vessel must not exceed 200 mm, the condition in
Equation \ref{eq:thin} can be shown to be satisfied:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{r}{t} = \frac{100}{4} = 25 > 10
    \label{eq:check_thin}
\end{equation}
Hence, the pressure vessel can be classified as thin-walled.\\[10pt]
The maximum pressure that the vessel can withstand is given by Equation
\ref{eq:hoop} since the maximum pressure from the hoop stress is lower than the
maximum pressure from the longitudinal stress.\\[10pt]
Hence, the maximum pressure that the vessel can withstand is given by:
\begin{equation}
    p_{max} = \frac{\sigma_1 t}{r}
    \label{eq:max_pressure}
\end{equation}
